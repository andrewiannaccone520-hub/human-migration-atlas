Corridors are now shown without modeled waypoint markers. New site additions are intended to be actual archaeological localities rather than assumed intermediate stops, while environmental overlays remain broad interpretive layers meant to keep sites and corridors visible underneath. In this version, glaciation is broken into multiple regional polygons, expands farther south during stronger phases, retreats clearly by 12k and 4k, and extends schematically back toward 4 MYA. Sea-level change is also expanded into multiple regional shelf polygons that vary by stage, including deep-time schematic coastal envelopes. North African humid periods / Green Sahara pulses are shown as repeating orbital-style interpretive climate layers tied directly to the timeline slider, and a major-volcanic-eruptions layer adds time-sensitive markers back toward 4 MYA. Cognitive transition zones now add broad time-bounded regions for symbolic, representational, and ritual expression as an interpretive research layer. A separate language-capacity layer now pairs non-geographic timeline thresholds for inferred Broca-like frontal reorganization, FOXP2 timing, and hyoid evidence with mapped anatomical evidence points and extended envelopes and three compact core nuclei plus two broader inferred extensions, while the broad interpretive population band remains removed in favor of more precise passes.

Project statement

This project is an independent educational and research visualization exploring human migration, climate, contact, and cognition through time. The map is designed as a research atlas: archaeological localities, environmental overlays, corridor hypotheses, population estimates, and symbolic/cognitive thresholds are shown together so that patterns can be examined spatially and chronologically.

Source and interpretation note

  • Archaeological sites represent published localities and approximate dates used for visualization.
  • Migration corridors, climate zones, population estimates, language-capacity regions, and cognitive transition zones are interpretive visual layers rather than precise archaeological boundaries.
  • Environmental overlays are intentionally transparent and schematic so that sites and routes remain visible.
  • Version v3.7 includes the 2026 evidence update noted in the sidebar and site popups.
